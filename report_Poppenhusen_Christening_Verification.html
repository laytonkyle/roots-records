Observations

  • Name discrepancies: The name index transcriptions frequently differ from the actual register entries (e.g., “Gunst Wilhelm Friedrich” in the index → “Paul Wilhelm Friedrich” in the register; “Anna Goffman Dorothea” → “Anna Christina Dorothea”). The index was likely compiled later from abbreviated or difficult-to-read entries.
  • Entry numbers may not match: The name index entry numbers don’t always correspond to the register (e.g., index listed Jürgen Nicolaus as e29, but the actual register entry was e58).
  • Mother’s full name: Entry 8 (1787) reveals the mother’s full name as Anna Maria Elisabeth geb. Schmidt — earlier entries abbreviated to just “Anna Maria.”
  • Occupation as identifier: W.F. Poppenhusen is consistently described as Bürger und Gastgeb (innkeeper), while Jürgen Poppenhusen is Bürger und Amts-Knecht (civil servant). This, combined with the mother’s name, provides reliable disambiguation.
  • Possible 3rd brother (entry 7): The father’s first name in entry 93 (p189) does not clearly read “Wilhelm” — another AI transcription suggested “Theodor.” The handwriting comparison against confirmed “Wilhelm” entries shows a different letter pattern. The occupation also reads “Amts-Knecht” rather than “Gastgeb.” However, the mother is “Anna Maria” (not Maria Magdalena), which doesn’t match Jürgen’s wife. This may represent a third Poppenhusen brother.
  • Third family discovered — Jürgen Wilhelm: Entries 13–21 (1808–1821) all belong to Jürgen Wilhelm Poppenhusen and wife Maria Margareta geb. Schütt. His occupation varies across entries: Handlungsgesell, Weißgermeister (master tanner). This is the same Jürgen Wilhelm known from the 1817 death record (daughter Maria Margaretha Ulrica, entry 16 here). He later remarried Wiebke Treshen.
  • Entry 12 bridges the gap: Paul Friedrich (1795) belongs to Jürgen (Amtsknecht) × Maria Magdalena Dibbern — giving him a 5th child and extending his family’s timeline from 1791 to 1795.
  • Jürgen Wilhelm’s changing occupations: Listed as Weißgermeister (tanner) in 1817 and 1821, but Handlungsgesell (trade journeyman) in 1819. Earlier records (1808–1815) just say “Bürger.” The 1817 death register called him “Ohlagesell” (oil worker).
  • Vol. 274918 page mapping: p24≈1795, p100≈1800, p160≈1805, p180≈1808, p210≈1812, p230≈1815, p253≈1817, p275≈1819, p301≈1821.
